Prior Learning Assessment (PLA)

Improving student outcomes by honoring learning from outside the classroom.

by kelsey anderson

Research on PLA has shown promising results for college outcomes

With ongoing public discourse around lack of affordability in postsecondary education and high unemployment rates resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic, innovations that reach adult learners and reduce college costs are timely. One such academic practice, Prior Learning Assessment (PLA), is already in widespread use and could have significant positive impacts.

PLA is when an institution of higher education recognizes learning that occured outside of the traditional college classroom and awards credit for it. This could be learning a student gained from work, serving in the military, taking online classes or any number of nontraditional sources.

Relatively little data about PLA exists, but a few studies have shown promising outcomes for students. This site applies findings from a 2019 study of PLA to nationwide student demographics to model what the difference between earning PLA credits or not could look like for someone's chances of earning a Bachelor's degree, how long it might take and how much it could cost.

Findings say it raises graduation rates by an average of 17 points, but impacts are even greater for some groups, like public two-year students.
